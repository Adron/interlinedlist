# What does "interlined" actually mean?

The word sits in the product name, but it has never been explained publicly. That feels like an omission worth correcting.

## The typographical root

**Interlineation** is the practice of writing or inserting text *between the existing lines* of a document. The term appears in manuscript traditions, legal practice, and scholarly annotation. When a scribe corrected an error, a lawyer amended a contract by hand, or a reader glossed a passage with their own commentary, they wrote in the space between the lines. The inserted text was called an interlineation. The act was interlineating.

The line breaks on a page were not just formatting. They were gaps — deliberate or incidental — that invited annotation, correction, elaboration. Structure grew in the margins and the whitespace, woven into the primary text without replacing it.

## How this maps to the product

Your messages are the primary stream. They flow in time order, one after another, each a moment recorded. That stream is the lines.

Lists, tags, documents, and structured data are interlined *within* that stream. They are not a separate system sitting alongside your messages. They emerge from the same chronological flow, woven between the everyday entries. A list you create on a Tuesday lives in the same timeline as the note you posted that morning. A document is not filed away somewhere else — it is threaded into the moments that produced it.

The structure does not replace the stream. It annotates it, the same way a medieval scribe annotated a manuscript page: in the space between.

## The time-series nature

Everything in InterlinedList flows chronologically. There is no hierarchy imposed from above — no folder tree you must populate before you can write, no category you must assign before you can start. You write, and the structure accumulates around what you have written.

This is the design principle the name encodes. The stream is primary. The lists, documents, and links that give it shape are the interlineations — inserted where they belong, between the moments that prompted them.

## Why this matters

Most tools start from structure: define the folders, then fill them. InterlinedList starts from the stream: write first, structure later. The name is not marketing. It is a commitment to that order of operations.

When you see "interlined," you are meant to picture the scribe's pen moving into the gap between two lines of an old text — not erasing what was there, not overwriting the original, just adding something that belongs precisely there, in that moment, between those lines.

That is what the platform does. That is what "interlined" means.
